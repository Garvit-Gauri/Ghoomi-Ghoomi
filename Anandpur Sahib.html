Anandpur Sahib

Introduction

Located in the western Indian state of Punjab, Anandpur Sahib is a sacred town receiving thousands of Sikh Pilgrims throughout the year. Best known for the countless gurudwaras or Sikh temples located here, it is bordered by the majestic Sutlej river on one side and the towering Shivaliks on another. The Anandpur Sahib Gurudwara is the primary reason for tourist interest in the place. However, both the bristling river and the snow-capped peaks add an aura of natural charm to the city.

History

Guru Tegh Bahadur arrived at Anandpur Sahib in the June of 1665 as he moved away from other Sikh sects residing in Kiratpur. About a decade after he moved to Anandpur Sahib, he died after intense persecution. His death was seen as martyrdom by his followers in the region and his son; Guru Gobind Singh became the tenth guru of Sikhs.
